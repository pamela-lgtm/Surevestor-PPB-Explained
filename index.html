What Is Landlord Shield Coverage?

SureVestor fills the gaps your homeowner's policy was never designed to cover

Your standard homeowner's insurance covers the building. It does not cover what happens when a tenant stops paying, causes intentional damage, or triggers a lawsuit. SureVestor's ProtectionPlus Bundle was built specifically for landlords — to cover exactly those scenarios.

Think of it as a second layer of protection that sits on top of your existing policy and activates the moment something goes wrong with your tenancy. It covers lost rent, legal costs, physical damage beyond the deposit, and liability exposure — all in one program.

Your Biggest Concerns — Addressed

Here's exactly how each scenario is covered

Most landlords come to us with one of four worries. Here's the full picture on each — including the edge cases and dollar amounts that matter most.

01
💸

Tenant Stops Paying Rent

Up to $3,000/mo · 25 weeks

Lost rent is among the most financially damaging landlord experiences — especially in Florida, where the eviction process takes 6–8 weeks minimum. Landlord Shield covers lost income from the point of default, not just after a judgment. It doesn't matter whether the tenant stops paying, skips out, or breaks the lease early.

  • Non-payment covered from the point of default — no waiting for a court order
  • Lease breaks and early abandonment covered — not just formal non-payment
  • Skipping (tenant leaves without notice) is a covered scenario
  • Coverage continues through the eviction process — the clock doesn't stop your income
  • Tenant death is also covered — an overlooked edge case that creates real hardship
  • Maximum: $3,000/month for up to 25 consecutive weeks
02
🏚️

Property Damage You Can't Recover

$100K accidental · $35K malicious

A security deposit — typically one month's rent — rarely covers the actual cost of serious tenant damage. Landlord Shield closes that gap significantly, covering both accidental and intentional damage well beyond what any deposit can absorb.

  • Accidental damage: fire, smoke, and water damage caused by tenant — $100K per occurrence
  • Malicious damage: intentional destruction — covered up to $35,000
  • Theft or damage from theft — covered up to $15,000
  • Coverage is independent of the tenant's ability to pay — it pays regardless
  • Lock rekeying after departure or eviction also covered
03
⚖️

Costly or Complicated Eviction

Up to $5,000 in eviction costs

Florida's eviction process is not fast and not cheap. The combination of attorney fees, court costs, sheriff fees, and lost rent during the process can easily exceed $5,000–$8,000 before resolution. Without coverage, you absorb every dollar. With Landlord Shield, most of it is reimbursed.

  • Legal eviction costs reimbursed up to $5,000
  • Sheriff service fees — $600 covered
  • Lock rekeying upon completion — covered
  • Lost rent during the eviction continues under the 25-week provision
  • Third-party liability: if the eviction becomes a lawsuit, $1M coverage activates
  • Context: at $3,000/month rent, a 7-week eviction = $5,250 in lost income alone before attorney fees
